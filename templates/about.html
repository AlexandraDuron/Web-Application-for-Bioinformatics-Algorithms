About

  • Mass Spectrometry

    Mass Spectrometry allows an accurate measurement of the mass of different molecules within a sample. Proteins which account for large biomolecules, are identifiable by mass. Thanks to Mass Spectrometry, it is possible to identify molecules in a mixture, detect impurities in a sample, analyze a purified protein, or study the protein content of a sample of cells.

    Mass Spectrometry, fragments molecules based on their chemical properties in the gas phase. Then,two related molecules are likely to display similar fragments of ion spectra.

    Molecular networking utilizes the chemistry of molecules to organize the data as a relational spectral network. This data organizational tool is applicable across diverse fields such as; the discovery of therapeutic leads, monitoring drug metabolism, clinical diagnostics, and emerging applications in precision medicine. [1]

  • Molecular Networking

    Molecular Networking utilizes filtering steps to make a clear separation between MS2 spectra clusters. In this process, only the most relevant similar scores are selected. According to the filtering values selected, some scores are arbitrarily deleted and part of the information is ignored. The selection of these values requires time- consuming parameters optimization.
